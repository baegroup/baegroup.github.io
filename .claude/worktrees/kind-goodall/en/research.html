Research

Bae Lab studies rechargeable battery systems with an emphasis on electrochemical mechanisms, practical design parameters, and data-enabled diagnostics.

Electrolyte Additive Development

Design and screening of additive chemistries for improved interface stability, cycle life, and high-rate performance.

Electrolyte Design for Rechargeable Batteries

Structure-property optimization of liquid and solid electrolytes for lithium-ion and all-solid-state battery platforms.

Battery Computational Simulation

Physics-based and multi-scale simulation to analyze transport, overpotential, and thermal behavior under practical operating conditions.

Battery Condition Diagnosis

Data-driven algorithms for SOC/SOH/SOP estimation, incipient short-circuit detection, and safety-oriented battery management.

Research Methodology

  • Electrochemical Experiment + Characterization Material synthesis, cell fabrication, and interface analysis.
  • Simulation + Mechanism Modeling Electrochemical and thermal coupled simulation for behavior prediction.
  • Machine Learning + Diagnostics Data-centric models for battery condition estimation and anomaly detection.
